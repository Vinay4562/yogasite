Yoga Asanas

Dhanurasana

Dhanurasana, commonly known as Bow Pose, is a yoga asana that involves lying on your stomach and lifting your chest and legs simultaneously, resembling the shape of a bow. This pose is part of the Hatha yoga tradition and is considered a backbend that helps open up the chest and improve spinal flexibility.

Pigeon Pose

Pigeon Pose, or Eka Pada Rajakapotasana, is a yoga pose that targets the hips and thighs. It's known for its ability to open up the hip flexors, improve flexibility, and release tension in the lower back and hips.

Warrior II

Warrior II Pose, or Virabhadrasana II, is a foundational yoga pose that is part of the standing pose category. This pose is named after Virabhadra, a mythical warrior created by the Hindu god Shiva. Warrior II is known for building strength, stability, and focus.

Wheel pose - Chakrasana

Wheel Pose, also known as Urdhva Dhanurasana in Sanskrit, is an invigorating and advanced yoga backbend that opens the chest, strengthens the arms and legs, and stretches the entire front side of the body. This pose is part of the backbending family of yoga asanas and is considered an intermediate to advanced posture.

Padangushthasana

Squat on the toes. The whole body is still and relaxed. Concentrate on a fixed point in front. Strengthens the ankle and foot muscles and is therefore beneficial for fallen arches and flat feet. Improves sense of balance and ability to concentrate.

Bakasana Pose - Crow Pose

Bakasana, commonly known as Crow Pose, is an arm balance pose in yoga that requires strength, balance, and focus. It's a foundational arm balance that is often introduced to intermediate practitioners. Bakasana is a great pose for building arm and core strength while improving concentration and coordination.
